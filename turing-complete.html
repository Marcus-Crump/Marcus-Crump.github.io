Self Teaching CS429 with "Turing Complete"

During my time in CS 313E (Elements of Software Design), a Data Structures and Algorithms course, I engaged in a conversation with my professor about avenues to further my education in computer science. Given my enthusiasm for games, my professor recommended Turing Complete, a game designed to teach computer architecture through a series of complex challenges. Intrigued by the concept, I explored the game and found its approach to be both engaging and informative. However, I recognized that while Turing Complete offers valuable insights, it might not encompass the full depth of knowledge expected from students in a formal computer architecture course at the University of Texas at Austin. To bridge this gap, I reviewed the syllabus for CS 429: Computer Organization and Architecture, a foundational course in UT's computer science curriculum . This led me to develop a comprehensive self-study plan aimed at mastering the course material and achieving a level of understanding comparable to that of enrolled students.

What is Turing Complete

Turing Complete is an educational puzzle game that guides players through the fundamentals of computer architecture by challenging them to build a functioning computer from the ground up. Starting with basic logic gates like NAND, players progressively construct more complex components—such as adders, memory units, and control circuits—culminating in the assembly of a programmable CPU. This hands-on approach offers an interactive and engaging way to explore digital logic, binary arithmetic, and low-level computing concepts.

The Plan

Primary Texts:

  • Computer Organization and Design by David A. Patterson and John L. Hennessy
  • Computer Systems: A Programmer's Perspective by Randal E. Bryant and David R. O'Hallaron
  • Modern C by Jens Gustedt
These texts are selected based on their inclusion in the syllabi of Dr. Chatterjee and Dr. Young, respectively. Dr. Young's course materials, including lecture slides and supplemental videos, will guide my weekly studies. Study Schedule:
  • Read one chapter from each textbook weekly
  • Read one chapter of Modern C a day, asjusting as necessary for chapter length and complexity
  • Review corresponding lecture slides and supplemental materials
  • Complete associated homework assignments
